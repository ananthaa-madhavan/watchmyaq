Air Quality Guide

Comparison of particulate matter types and health effects.

PM1

Definition: Ultra-fine particles (<1 micron)

Sources:
Combustion, wildfire smoke, industrial emissions

Health Effects:
Can penetrate deep into lungs and bloodstream. Linked to systemic inflammation.

PM2.5

Definition: Fine inhalable particles

Sources:
Vehicles, power plants, burning fuel

Health Effects:
Asthma, reduced lung function, cardiovascular strain

PM10

Definition: Coarse particles (<10 microns)

Sources:
Dust, pollen, construction sites

Health Effects:
Nose/throat irritation, respiratory discomfort
