Air Quality Guide

Understanding particulate matter and how it affects human health.

PM1

Extremely fine particles that can penetrate deep into the lungs and even enter the bloodstream.

Sources: combustion, wildfire smoke

PM2.5

Fine inhalable particles associated with respiratory and cardiovascular issues.

Sources: vehicles, industrial emissions

PM10

Larger inhalable particles that can irritate the nose, throat, and upper airways.

Sources: dust, pollen, construction

Health Effects Overview

Exposure to elevated particulate matter levels can contribute to asthma, reduced lung function, and long-term cardiovascular risks. Sensitive groups include children, the elderly, and individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions.
